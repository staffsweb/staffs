Typography

Typeface

Two typefaces are used for the web

  • Arial - Primary typeface: is the default for body copy
  • Museo Sans - Secondary typeface: for headings and other 'decorative' copy.

They can be used using a class of font-primary or font-secondary
or in Sass using @include font-primary; or @include font-secondary;

Typescale

The following sizes are defined in __vars/_typography.scss to set a typescale.

This will only change the font-size

  • 6xl: The biggest font-size 4 (rem)
  • 5xl: The second biggest size 3 (rem)
  • 4xl: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 2.5 (rem)
  • 3xl: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 2 (rem)
  • 2xl: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 1.5 (rem)
  • xl: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 1.25 (rem)
  • lg: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 1.125 (rem)
  • md: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 1 (rem)
  • sm: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 0.875 (rem)
  • xs: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 0.75 (rem)
  • 2xs: The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog 0.6875 (rem)

They can be used using a class of font-[sizename], eg. font-2xl
or in Sass using @include typescale('[sizename]');, eg. @include typescale('xs');

Heading Levels

Use semantic heading level elements (<h1>-<h6>) for content, do not use these elements for their visual appearance.

They can be sized by applying a class above, eg. <h2 class="font-xl">.

To make a non-heading element appear in a heading style (including font-family), a class of h1-h6 can be applied. eg. <div class="h3">

Headings are assigned one of the Typescale sizes in __vars/_typography.scss.

h1 Default: 5xl
h2 Default: 4xl
h3 Default: 3xl
h4 Default: 2xl
h5 Default: xl
h6 Default: lg
